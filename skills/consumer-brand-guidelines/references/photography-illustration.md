# Photography & Illustration

Source: Zillow April 2024 Brand Guidelines, slides 167-252.

## House Motifs

The House Motif is the primary graphic device that expresses the Zillow brand beyond the logo. Derived from the logomark.

### Three Motif Types

| Motif | Purpose | When to Use |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Frame** | Leading motif; shows broad plane of photography | Dynamic photography, telling larger stories, bringing color + life |
| **Window** | Draws focus; highlights intimate moments | Close-up moments, joy, excitement; movement that breaks through the shape |
| **Solid House** | Graphic expression without photography | Typography-focused layouts, limited size/aspect ratios, challenging placements |

### Frame Rules
- Always appears as a cropped element
- Lower edge/curve must not be visible when cropping with images
- Peak and at least one vertical edge must be visible
- Width of frame equals the safe area to the border
- Use to bring characters in the scene together

### Window Rules
- Peak and at least one vertical axis must always be visible
- Movement and energy should overlap the crop (parts of image extend beyond shape)
- When cropped at bottom, curved lower corner must not be visible
- People should not be completely contained — part of image must extend beyond

### Solid House Rules
- Center-align all typography within the solid house
- Scale proportionally; never stretch or warp
- Can be cropped abstractly but keep the apex intact
- Zillow logo OK inside solid house only when cropped abstractly

### When to Skip the House Motif
- Small placements and challenging aspect ratios
- Wide angle photography
- Educational, informational tone (not emotional)
- Secondary email placements (not hero)

### House Motif DON'Ts (All Types)
- Do not crop the apex
- Do not display as uncropped element
- Do not separate or exclude people
- Do not use with images without people
- Do not use with wide angle photography or home exteriors (Frame/Window)
- Do not crop phones, laptops, or non-person objects within the motif
- Do not manipulate or stretch the shape
- Do not change the shape

## Illustration

### Two Types

| Type | Size | Use Cases |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Scene** | 300×500px aspect ratio | Landing page upsells, social media, email headers, blog headers, wizard intro pages |
| **Spot** | 160×160px | Value prop lists, email lists, product upsell banners, empty states, feature cards |

### Visual Characteristics
- Pattern and texture in clothing for dimension and friendliness
- Organic forms (combination of rounded and square edges)
- Textured hand-drawn line work for sounds and movement
- Balanced warm and cool tones from the Zillow palette
- Beige background element (#FFF0E1) to ground spot illustrations
- Friendly, approachable facial features

### Illustration Color Palette

Uses the Zillow primary + secondary palette, plus:

| Name | Hex | Use |
|------|-----|-----|
| Beige | `#FFF0E1` | Background element to ground illustrations |
| Yellow | `#F08B00` | Finance/money representations |
| Light Yellow | `#FFC752` | Finance accents |

### Color Strategy for Illustrations
- **Zillow Blue presence:** 10-50% in every illustration for brand identification
- **Granite as unifier:** 5-25% for depth (eyes, folds in clothing, hair color)
- **Finance = green:** Money and dollar signs always in green palette
- **Balanced color:** Warm and cool tones; strategic highlight and shadow palettes
- **Tonal textures:** Patterns appear tonally (e.g., Pool Blue on Waterfront Blue) for texture without distraction

### Illustration DON'Ts

| DON'T | DO Instead |
|-------|-----------|
| Use colors outside the illustration palette | Use full breadth of the approved palette |
| Use unnatural skin tones or hair colors | Reference the skin and hair color palettes |
| Edit, embellish, or remove elements | Contact the illustration team for requests |
| Remove or change the beige background element | Keep it for visual consistency |
| Layer colors with insufficient contrast | Ensure contrast between focal elements and backgrounds |
| Pair colors that create visual vibration | Use tonal contrast or complementary colors |
| Create new illustrations without approval | Contact the illustration team |
| Omit Zillow Blue from any composition | Use it strategically in all compositions |

### Skin Tone Palette
- Five primary skin tones (P1-P5) with secondary tones (S1-S5) for facial detail
- Eyes are always Granite, regardless of skin tone
- No unnatural skin colors
- Fireplace Orange may be used as lip color

### Hair Color Palette
- Seven tones including three brown tones from skin palette
- Natural colors only; no unnatural hair colors

### Human Forms
- Inclusive: range of skin tones, hairstyles, ages, cultural backgrounds, abilities, family dynamics
- Pattern and color in clothing for energy and personality
- Implied movement through hand-drawn line work
- Clothing folds in Granite (lighter tonal folds on dark backgrounds)

## Photography

### Art Direction
- Dynamic energy and unmissable joy
- Movement (real or suggested) even in moments of relaxation
- Relatable rather than staged
- Vibrant, rich, saturated colors matching the brand refresh
- Natural light, shot in real homes

### Casting & Inclusivity
- Diversity in ethnicity, background, religion, style, body type, culture, socioeconomic class
- Customers should see themselves reflected in imagery

### Wardrobe
- Authentic, lived-in clothing (not neutral, off-the-rack)
- Photogenic pops of color for joy and optimism
- Each outfit unique to the character
- Trendy without dating quickly

### Props & Interiors
- Authentic, lived-in styling (hand-me-down > brand new)
- Eclectic, unique artwork (not catalog-generic)
- Pops of color in harmony with wardrobe
- Personal touches: well-tended garden, children's bikes, bird feeder

### Color Treatment
- Fresh, vibrant, with snap and sparkle
- Warm side of neutral (stop short of yellow cast)
- Vibrant, believably saturated
- Shadows can fall off for contrast; highlights retain some tone
- Windows bring in a hint of outdoors; skies feel believable with some blue

### AI Guidelines
- No wholly AI-generated images for consumer-facing assets
- AI OK for: geographic diversity, aspect ratio adjustment, subtle manipulation for type placement
- Never manipulate faces or hands with AI
- Never add additional people to a scene
- AI use should be undetectable
- Use only Zillow-approved AI tools (Adobe Firefly)
- Midjourney, DALL-E, etc. cannot be used for consumer-facing assets (legal/image rights)
