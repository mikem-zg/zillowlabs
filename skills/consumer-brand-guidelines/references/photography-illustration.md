## Marketing: House Motifs

The House Motif is the primary graphic device that expresses the Zillow brand beyond the logo. Derived from the logomark. Used in marketing contexts: ads, email, social, landing pages, OOH.

### Three Motif Types

| Motif | Purpose | When to Use |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Frame** | Leading motif; shows broad plane of photography | Dynamic photography, telling larger stories, bringing color + life |
| **Window** | Draws focus; highlights intimate moments | Close-up moments, joy, excitement; movement that breaks through the shape |
| **Solid House** | Graphic expression without photography | Typography-focused layouts, limited size/aspect ratios, challenging placements |

### Frame Rules
- Always appears as a cropped element
- Lower edge/curve must not be visible when cropping with images
- Peak and at least one vertical edge must be visible
- Width of frame equals the safe area to the border
- Use to bring characters in the scene together (connection and joy)

### Window Rules
- Peak and at least one vertical axis must always be visible
- Movement and energy should overlap the crop (parts of image extend beyond shape)
- When cropped at bottom, curved lower corner must not be visible
- People should not be completely contained — part of image must extend beyond

### Solid House Rules
- Center-align all typography within the solid house
- Scale proportionally; never stretch or warp
- Can be cropped abstractly but keep the apex intact
- Zillow logo OK inside solid house only when cropped abstractly
- Use "dark / shadow backgrounds" sparingly

### When to Skip the House Motif
- Small placements and challenging aspect ratios
- Wide angle photography
- Educational, informational tone (not emotional)
- Secondary email placements (not hero)

### House Motif DON'Ts (All Types)
- Do not crop the apex
- Do not display as uncropped element
- Do not separate or exclude people
- Do not use with images without people
- Do not use with wide angle photography or home exteriors (Frame/Window)
- Do not crop phones, laptops, or non-person objects within the motif
- Do not manipulate or stretch the shape
- Do not change the shape
- Do not place the window on a background color other than white

## Product: Shape, Containers & Elevation

Product contexts: app UI, website screens, in-product flows.

### House Motifs in Product
The Frame and Window can be used as hero images on landing pages and in upsells across all LOBs. Follow the same marketing usage guidelines for construction.

### Containers in Product

| Type | Appearance | Use Case | Importance Level |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Filled (saturated)** | Solid bold color | Upsells, alerts, system notifications | Highest — most important element on page |
| **Filled (white)** | White background | Section callouts, accordions | Moderate |
| **Outlined** | Border, no fill | Grouping form fields, discrete sections | Clear separation |
| **Filled + Elevated** | Shadow + fill | Interactive cards, property cards, task cards | Interactive element |
| **Outlined + Elevated** | Shadow + border | Interactive cards needing more contrast | Interactive element |

### Accessible Interactive Containers
- Interactive cards must have a secondary visual indicator communicating interactivity (text, border, or shadow)
- If a container has text inside plus a border or shadow, the border/shadow doesn't need to separately meet contrast requirements
- Cards without any secondary indicator are not recommended — users with visual disabilities may not know they are clickable

### Rounded Corners in Product

| Type | Radius | Use Cases | Min Padding |
|------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Default** | 12px | Buttons, small cards, property cards, banners, alerts | 8-16px |
| **Large** | 20px | Prominent hero cards, large sections | 16-32px |
| **Full** (∞) | Pill | Tags, chips, icon buttons | — |
| **Circular** | 50% | Small interactive elements | — |

**Rules:**
- Do NOT override Constellation component corner radii
- Nested rounded corners: outer container must have equal or larger rounding than inner
- Large rounded corners with small padding looks juvenile — increase padding or use 12px
- 12px spacing is the standard baseline for most component padding

### Elevation in Product

| Shadow | Specs | Use Cases |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| **Large** | Blur 30px, Y 15px, 15% opacity | Property cards, toggle cards, sizable UI — most exciting/joyful shadow |
| **Medium** | Blur 20px, Y 7px, 15% opacity | Intermediate components, sliding backgrounds (e.g., HDP) |
| **Small heavy** | Blur 15px, Y 6px, 30% opacity | Interactive chips on maps, text/icons on photos |
| **Small light** | Blur 15px, Y 4px, 15% opacity | Small interactive components — use the least, never on large cards |

**Rules:**
- Large dispersed shadow is preferred whenever possible (most joyful)
- Shadows indicate interactivity — use them to guide customers to clickable elements
- Not all interactive elements need shadows (e.g., buttons typically do not)
- Do NOT nest shadows inside other shadows
- Do NOT use shadows on static, non-interactive elements

### Dark Mode Elevation
- No shadows in dark mode
- Use lighter backgrounds to portray elevation (like shining a flashlight from above)
- Lightest areas = closest to user; darkest areas = farthest away

## Illustration (Both Marketing and Product)

### Two Types

| Type | Size | Marketing Use | Product Use |
|------|------|---------------|-------------|
| **Scene** | 300×500px aspect ratio | Landing page upsells, social media, email headers, blog headers, wizard intro pages | Landing page heroes, upsells |
| **Spot** | 160×160px | Email lists, value prop sets | Product upsell banners, empty states, feature cards |

### Visual Characteristics
- Pattern and texture in clothing for dimension and friendliness
- Organic forms (combination of rounded and square edges)
- Textured hand-drawn line work for sounds and movement
- Balanced warm and cool tones from the Zillow palette
- Beige background element (#FFF0E1) to ground spot illustrations
- Friendly, approachable facial features

### Illustration Color Palette

Uses the Zillow primary + secondary palette, plus:

| Name | Hex | Use |
|------|-----|-----|
| Beige | `#FFF0E1` | Background element to ground illustrations |
| Yellow | `#F08B00` | Finance/money representations |
| Light Yellow | `#FFC752` | Finance accents |

### Color Strategy for Illustrations
- **Zillow Blue presence:** 10-50% in every illustration for brand identification
- **Granite as unifier:** 5-25% for depth (eyes, folds in clothing, hair color)
- **Finance = green:** Money and dollar signs always in green palette
- **Balanced color:** Warm and cool tones; strategic highlight and shadow palettes
- **Tonal textures:** Patterns appear tonally (e.g., Pool Blue on Waterfront Blue)

### Illustration DON'Ts

| DON'T | DO Instead |
|-------|-----------|
| Use colors outside the illustration palette | Use full breadth of the approved palette |
| Use unnatural skin tones or hair colors | Reference the skin and hair color palettes |
| Edit, embellish, or remove elements | Contact the illustration team for requests |
| Remove or change the beige background element | Keep it for visual consistency |
| Layer colors with insufficient contrast | Ensure contrast between focal elements and backgrounds |
| Pair colors that create visual vibration | Use tonal contrast or complementary colors |
| Create new illustrations without approval | Contact the illustration team |
| Omit Zillow Blue from any composition | Use it strategically in all compositions |

### Skin Tone Palette
- Five primary skin tones (P1-P5) with secondary tones (S1-S5) for facial detail
- Eyes are always Granite, regardless of skin tone
- No unnatural skin colors
- Fireplace Orange may be used as lip color

### Hair Color Palette
- Seven tones including three brown tones from skin palette
- Natural colors only; no unnatural hair colors

### Human Forms
- Inclusive: range of skin tones, hairstyles, ages, cultural backgrounds, abilities, family dynamics
- Pattern and color in clothing for energy and personality
- Implied movement through hand-drawn line work
- Clothing folds in Granite (lighter tonal folds on dark backgrounds)

## Product: Iconography

### Icon Sizes

| Size | Pixels | Use |
|------|--------|-----|
| **Small** | 16px | Constellation components, input fields, paired with Body Small text |
| **Medium** | 24px | Default size; pairs with Body and Body Bold text |
| **Large** | 32px | Higher emphasis moments (gleams, content blocks in modals); max 5 in proximity |
| **X-Large** | 44px | Where a spot illustration adds too much visual prominence; max 3 in proximity |

### Icon Styles

| Style | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Filled** (default) | Most use cases; complements bold type and visual refresh direction |
| **Outline** | Pre-interaction states (e.g., unfavorited heart), very dense task-oriented UIs |

### Icon Colors in Product

| Context | Color | When |
|---------|-------|------|
| Most interactive icons | **Blue600** | Default for clickable icons |
| Functional icons in containers | **Gray950** | Inside input fields, tabbed nav, CTA containers, global nav |
| Content support | **Gray600** | Paired with content for separation/prominence |
| Storytelling emphasis | **Teal600** | Features, landing pages, upsell banners |
| Vibrant non-interactive | **Orange500**, etc. | Prominent moments like upsell banners; use sparingly |

**Note:** Use vibrant xl icons instead of legacy "detailed icons." Consider whether a spot illustration would serve the purpose better.

## Photography (Both Marketing and Product)

### Art Direction
- Dynamic energy and unmissable joy
- Movement (real or suggested) even in moments of relaxation
- Relatable rather than staged
- Vibrant, rich, saturated colors matching the brand refresh
- Natural light, shot in real homes

### Casting & Inclusivity
- Diversity in ethnicity, background, religion, style, body type, culture, socioeconomic class
- Customers should see themselves reflected in imagery

### Wardrobe
- Authentic, lived-in clothing (not neutral, off-the-rack)
- Photogenic pops of color for joy and optimism
- Each outfit unique to the character
- Trendy without dating quickly

### Props & Interiors
- Authentic, lived-in styling (hand-me-down > brand new)
- Eclectic, unique artwork (not catalog-generic)
- Pops of color in harmony with wardrobe
- Personal touches: well-tended garden, children's bikes, bird feeder

### Color Treatment
- Fresh, vibrant, with snap and sparkle
- Warm side of neutral (stop short of yellow cast)
- Vibrant, believably saturated
- Shadows can fall off for contrast; highlights retain some tone
- Windows bring in a hint of outdoors; skies feel believable with some blue

### AI Guidelines
- No wholly AI-generated images for consumer-facing assets
- AI OK for: geographic diversity, aspect ratio adjustment, subtle manipulation for type placement
- Never manipulate faces or hands with AI
- Never add additional people to a scene
- AI use should be undetectable
- Use only Zillow-approved AI tools (Adobe Firefly)
- Midjourney, DALL-E, etc. cannot be used for consumer-facing assets (legal/image rights)

### Product: Native Platform Color Balance
60-30-10 rule for small screens: 60% dominant color, 30% secondary color, 10% accent color. Ensures well-balanced layouts.

### Product: Native vs Custom UI
Native design (platform navigation) ensures familiarity and performance. Custom design (property cards, branded elements) offers unique brand experiences. Prioritize usability.
